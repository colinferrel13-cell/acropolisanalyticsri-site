Research projects

Selected and emerging lines of work anchored in the Population-Adjusted Epidemiology Outcomes Network (PAEON) and related analytic frameworks.

PAEON — Population-Adjusted Epidemiology Outcomes Network

A modular backbone for harmonizing survey, surveillance, and population data to support county- and region-level chronic disease, cognitive decline, and access-to-care modeling.

  • PAEON I — PAX: U.S.–Mexico border chronic disease, infectious disease, and crime burden mapping.
  • PAEON II — AORTA: Cardiometabolic and cardiovascular risk and burden, with emphasis on small-area estimates.
  • PAEON V — MNEME: Cognitive decline, dementia-like burden, and caregiver strain.
  • PAEON VII — HYGEIA: Physical activity, screen-time clustering, and youth risk-behavior patterns.

These “Labors” can be adapted to Texas-specific questions or extended nationally for grant proposals and cross-jurisdictional comparisons.

Texas-focused chronic disease & ADRD surveillance

Methodological work to improve state-level and sub-state surveillance for chronic disease and Alzheimer’s & related dementias (ADRD), including:

  • Refining small-area estimates for high-burden regions
  • Designing trend and disparity analyses tailored to state priorities
  • Developing staging frameworks for program evaluation and planning
